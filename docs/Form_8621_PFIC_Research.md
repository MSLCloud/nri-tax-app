# IRS Form 8621 PFIC Reporting Requirements — Research Summary

**Sources:** IRS Form 8621 Instructions (12/2025), IRS.gov, Form 8621 PDF, Publication 550 references

---

## 1. PFIC Election Options Available

Form 8621 provides **eight election options** in Part II:

| Election | Code Section | Description |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **A. QEF Election** | §1295 | Treat the PFIC as a Qualified Electing Fund. Requires PFIC to provide Annual Information Statement with ordinary earnings and net capital gain. |
| **B. Extend Time for Payment of Tax** | §1294 | Defer tax on undistributed QEF earnings until election terminates. Interest accrues on deferred amount. |
| **C. Mark-to-Market (MTM)** | §1296 | Mark PFIC stock to market annually if stock is "marketable." Gains/losses reported in Part IV. |
| **D. Deemed Sale (with QEF)** | §1291(d)(2)(A) | For first-day shareholders when PFIC becomes QEF. Recognize gain on deemed sale at qualification date. |
| **E. Deemed Dividend (with QEF)** | §1291(d)(2)(B) | For PFICs that are also CFCs. Treat post-1986 E&P as excess distribution on qualification date. |
| **F. Deemed Sale (Former PFIC)** | §1298(b)(1) | For former PFICs or §1297(e) PFICs. Purge prior PFIC gain via deemed sale at termination/qualification date. |
| **G. Deemed Dividend (§1297(e) PFIC)** | §1298(b)(1) | For §1297(e) PFICs (CFC overlap). Treat post-1986 E&P as excess distribution on CFC qualification date. |
| **H. Deemed Dividend (Former PFIC)** | §1298(b)(1) | For former PFICs that were CFCs. Treat post-1986 E&P as excess distribution on termination date. |

**Additional:** Qualifying Insurance Corporation election (alternative facts and circumstances test under §1297(f)(2)) — limited-information Form 8621.

**Late elections:** Form 8621-A for purging elections made after the 3-year period (additional interest due).

## 2. Required Fields for Each Election

### Part I — Summary of Annual Information (Required for most filers)

| Line | Field | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Description of each class of shares | |
| 2 | Date shares acquired during tax year | If applicable |
| 3 | Number of shares at end of tax year | |
| 4 | Value of shares at end of tax year | Ranges: $0–50K, $50K–100K, etc., or exact if >$200K |
| 5 | Type of PFIC and amounts | §1291, §1293 (QEF), §1296 (MTM) |

**Exceptions to Part I:** $25K aggregate PFIC value, $5K indirect PFIC value, exempt orgs, certain trusts/estates.

---

### Election A — QEF (§1295)

- **Part II:** Check box A
- **Part III:** Lines 6a–7c (and 8a–9c if Election B)
  - 6a: Pro rata share of ordinary earnings
  - 6b: Portion included under §951 or excluded under §1293(g)
  - 6c: Ordinary income (6a − 6b)
  - 7a: Pro rata share of net capital gain
  - 7b: Portion under §951 or §1293(g)
  - 7c: Net long-term capital gain (7a − 7b)
- **Documentation:** PFIC Annual Information Statement or Annual Intermediary Statement (or Combined statement)

---

### Election B — Extend Time for Payment

- **Part II:** Check box B
- **Part III:** Lines 8a–9c
  - 8a–8e: Undistributed earnings calculation
  - 9a–9c: Deferred tax calculation
- **Part VI:** Annual status reporting (lines 17–26)

---

### Election C — Mark-to-Market (§1296)

- **Part II:** Check box C
- **Part IV:** Lines 10a–14c
  - 10a: FMV of PFIC stock at year-end
  - 10b: Adjusted basis at year-end
  - 10c: Gain or (loss)
  - 11: Unreversed inclusions
  - 12: Loss (limited to unreversed inclusions)
  - 13a–14c: Dispositions during year (FMV, basis, gain/loss, unreversed inclusions)
- **First year:** May need Part V instead (coordination with §1291)

---

### Election D — Deemed Sale (with QEF)

- **Part II:** Check box D
- **Part V:** Line 15f (gain or loss), Line 16 (tax and interest if gain)

---

### Election E — Deemed Dividend (with QEF, CFC)

- **Part II:** Check box E
- **Part V:** Line 15e(2) (excess distribution), Line 16
- **Attachment:** Statement showing pro rata share of post-1986 E&P calculation

---

### Elections F, G, H — Former PFIC / §1297(e) PFIC

- **Part II:** Check box F, G, or H
- **Part V:** Line 15f (F) or 15e(2) (G, H), Line 16
- **Attachments (G, H):** Statement with E&P calculation, termination/qualification date, election year dates, Schedule J (Form 5471) if applicable

---

### Part V — Section 1291 Fund (Excess Distributions / Dispositions)

| Line | Field |
|------|-------|
| Currency code | 3-letter code (above 15a) |
| 15a | Total distributions in current year |
| 15b | Distributions in prior 3 years (reduced) |
| 15c | 15b ÷ 3 |
| 15d | 15c × 1.25 |
| 15e(1) | Excess distribution (15a − 15d) |
| 15e(2) | Amount in USD |
| 15f | Gain/loss on disposition |
| 16a–16f | Allocation, tax, interest, FTC |

## 3. Cost Basis Tracking Requirements

### QEF (Election A)

- Basis **increased** by earnings included in gross income
- Basis **decreased** by distributions to extent of previously taxed amounts
- Per-share or per-block tracking for different holding periods
- PFIC Annual Information Statement provides data for pro rata share

### Mark-to-Market (Election C)

- Basis **increased** by amounts included in income (mark-to-market gains)
- Basis **decreased** by amounts allowed as deduction (mark-to-market losses)
- **Unreversed inclusions** = prior-year inclusions minus prior-year deductions (limits loss recognition)
- Must track: FMV at year-end, adjusted basis, unreversed inclusions

### Section 1291 Fund (No QEF/MTM)

- **Per-share or per-block** basis for excess distribution allocation
- Excess distribution allocated to each day in holding period
- Gain on disposition = excess distribution (entire gain)
- Basis increased by gain recognized on deemed sale (Elections D, F)

### Deemed Sale / Deemed Dividend Elections

- Basis increased by gain recognized (Deemed Sale) or deemed dividend amount (Deemed Dividend)
- New holding period begins at qualification/termination date

### Key Rule

Cost basis must be maintained **per share or per block** (same acquisition date) for:
- Excess distribution calculations (125% test, allocation)
- MTM gain/loss and unreversed inclusions
- QEF basis adjustments

## 4. IRS Rules on Historical NAV Data

### Explicit Guidance

The Form 8621 instructions do **not** prescribe specific rules for historical NAV data. They focus on:

1. **Value of shares (Part I, Line 4):**  
   Shareholders may rely on **periodic account statements provided at least annually** unless they have actual knowledge or reason to know the statements do not reflect a reasonable estimate of the PFIC’s value.

2. **Fair market value (MTM):**  
   Part IV requires FMV of PFIC stock at year-end and on disposition dates. No specific NAV methodology is mandated.

3. **Asset test (PFIC status):**  
   Reg. §1.1297-1: Publicly traded foreign corporations must use **fair market value** for the asset test. Non–publicly traded may use adjusted basis in certain cases.

### Practical Implications

- **MTM:** Need FMV at year-end and on disposition dates. For marketable PFICs, this is typically last sale price or closing price on a qualified exchange.
- **Section 1291:** Need distributions for current and prior 3 years to compute excess distribution.
- **QEF:** PFIC must provide ordinary earnings and net capital gain (or data to compute them); NAV is not directly required by Form 8621.
- **Historical data:** No explicit requirement to retain historical NAV. However, for:
  - Excess distribution allocation (daily allocation over holding period)
  - MTM unreversed inclusions (prior-year history)
  - Basis adjustments over time  
  taxpayers need sufficient records to support amounts reported.

### Publication 550

Publication 550 covers investment income and capital gains generally. It does **not** contain detailed PFIC rules; Form 8621 instructions and related regulations are the primary authority.

## 5. Detailed Outline for Form 8621 Compliance

### A. Data Model & Storage

1. **PFIC Master**
   - PFIC identifier (EIN or Reference ID)
   - Name, address, tax year
   - PFIC type: QEF, §1291 fund, MTM, Former PFIC, §1297(e) PFIC
   - Chain-of-ownership flag (direct vs indirect)

2. **Shareholder / Ownership**
   - Shareholder ID, type (individual, corp, partnership, etc.)
   - Ownership structure (direct, through pass-through, through foreign entity)
   - Joint ownership flag

3. **Share Lots / Blocks**
   - PFIC ID, shareholder ID
   - Class of shares
   - Acquisition date
   - Number of shares
   - Cost basis (initial)
   - Holding period start (consider qualification/termination dates for purging elections)

4. **Annual Snapshots**
   - Year-end share count
   - Year-end FMV or value range
   - Year-end adjusted basis
   - Unreversed inclusions (MTM)

5. **Distributions**
   - Date, amount, currency
   - PFIC, shareholder, share block
   - Classification: excess vs non-excess (for §1291)

6. **QEF Data**
   - Ordinary earnings (pro rata)
   - Net capital gain (pro rata)
   - §951 / §1293(g) adjustments
   - PFIC Annual Information Statement storage

7. **Elections**
   - Election type (A–H)
   - Election year
   - Status (active, terminated, revoked)

---

### B. Business Logic

1. **Filing Requirement Engine**
   - Determine if Form 8621 is required (distributions, dispositions, QEF/MTM reporting, elections, §1298(f))
   - Apply exceptions ($25K, $5K, exempt org, trust rules)
   - Identify “first U.S. person” in chain for Part I

2. **Election Eligibility**
   - QEF: PFIC provides required statement
   - MTM: Stock is “marketable” per §1296(e) and Reg. §1.1296-2
   - Deemed Sale/Dividend: First-day shareholder, CFC status, former PFIC status

3. **Excess Distribution Calculation (§1291)**
   - Current-year distributions (15a)
   - Prior 3 years’ distributions, reduced (15b)
   - 125% test (15c, 15d)
   - Excess = 15a − 15d
   - Currency conversion to USD (spot rate on distribution date)

4. **Excess Distribution Allocation**
   - Allocate to each day in holding period
   - Split by tax year (current, pre-PFIC, PFIC years)
   - Compute tax increase per PFIC year (highest §1 or §11 rate)
   - Compute interest (IRC §6621) from due date of return for that year to due date of current return

5. **MTM Calculations**
   - Year-end: FMV − basis = gain or loss
   - Loss limited to unreversed inclusions
   - Dispositions: FMV and basis on disposition date, same loss limitation
   - Track unreversed inclusions across years

6. **QEF Calculations**
   - Ordinary income = 6a − 6b
   - Net capital gain = 7a − 7b
   - Section 1294: Undistributed earnings, deferred tax, interest

7. **Basis Adjustments**
   - QEF: + inclusions, − distributions (previously taxed)
   - MTM: + gains included, − losses deducted
   - Deemed Sale: + gain recognized
   - Deemed Dividend: + deemed dividend

---

### C. Form Assembly & Output

1. **Part I**
   - Lines 1–5 from data model
   - Value ranges or exact value

2. **Part II**
   - Check applicable election boxes

3. **Part III** (QEF)
   - Lines 6a–7c, optionally 8a–9c

4. **Part IV** (MTM)
   - Lines 10a–12 (year-end)
   - Lines 13a–14c (dispositions)
   - Handle multiple dispositions with attachments

5. **Part V** (§1291)
   - Separate Part V per excess distribution / disposition block
   - Currency code, 15a–15f, 16a–16f
   - Attach allocation statement

6. **Part VI** (§1294)
   - Lines 17–26 for prior-year elections and terminations

7. **Attachments**
   - PFIC Annual Information Statements
   - E&P calculation statements (Elections E, G, H)
   - Allocation statements for Part V
   - Protective statements (retroactive QEF)

---

### D. Validation & Compliance

1. **Reference ID**
   - Alphanumeric, ≤50 chars, consistent year-over-year
   - Correlation for mergers, entity classification changes

2. **Documentation Retention**
   - All Forms 8621, attachments, PFIC/Intermediary statements
   - Failure to produce can invalidate/terminate QEF election

3. **Timing**
   - Elections by due date (including extensions) of return
   - Retroactive QEF: Protective statement or IRS consent
   - Deemed Sale/Dividend: Original or amended return within 3 years

4. **Currency**
   - Excess distribution in single currency (USD or foreign)
   - Spot rate on distribution date for conversion

5. **Tax Rate Table**
   - Maintain historical §1 and §11 rates for excess distribution allocation (e.g., 1987–2025)

---

### E. Integration Points

1. **Form 8938**
   - Excepted Specified Foreign Financial Assets checkbox
   - Coordination with Form 8621 count on Form 8938

2. **Form 5471**
   - Schedule J for Elections G, H when applicable

3. **Form 1040 / 1120**
   - Ordinary income, capital gain, other income
   - Section 1291 additional tax and interest
   - Section 1294 deferred tax and interest

4. **Form 8621-A**
   - Late purging elections (after 3 years)

---

### F. Edge Cases to Handle

- Multiple share blocks with different holding periods
- Chain of ownership (separate Form 8621 per PFIC in chain)
- First year of MTM (possible §1291 overlap)
- Unpedigreed QEF (subject to §1291 on distributions)
- Partial termination of §1294 election
- Protective statement for retroactive QEF
- CFC overlap (§1297(d)) and §1297(e) PFIC
- Indirect ownership through pass-through entities
